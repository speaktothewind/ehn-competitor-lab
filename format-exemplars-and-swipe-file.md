# Format Exemplars + Viral Swipe File (out-of-niche "format school")

These are **not competitors** and **not your niche.** They're credentialed, high-engagement creators who've already solved the problem you're chasing: *being an authority AND going viral.* You **borrow their structure, hooks and mechanics — never their topic.** Substance stays 100% in your lane (gut, methylation, thyroid, fatigue, toxins); packaging comes from here.

Why these and not "trainers": a clinician's trust is the conversion asset. The best models are other **credentialed experts who went viral** — dermatologists, doctor-debunkers, and finance explainers (masters of making a dry, anxiety-laden topic feel urgent). Trainers skew aesthetic/bro and risk your credibility, so they're not in this set.

**KPI reminder:** the goal isn't raw views — it's *qualified* reach (saves, shares, DMs from health-intent viewers). Don't copy cheap-dopamine formats that pull the wrong audience and pollute your targeting.

## The Swipe File — viral formats → ready-to-shoot EHN posts

Each row: the pattern, who does it best, and a version filled with *your* substance.

1. **"X things I'd never do as a [role]"** (Idriss, Shah) → *"3 supplements I'd never take as a functional medicine practitioner — and what I use instead."*
2. **Myth-bust cold open** "Everything you've been told about X is wrong" (Idz, Rajan) → *"Everything you've been told about bloating is wrong. It's usually not food intolerance."*
3. **Doctor-reacts to a viral trend** (Mike, Rajan) → green-screen react to a viral "parasite cleanse" / "liver detox" reel and calmly break down what's real.
4. **Stat-shock hook** (Rajan, Idz) → *"Up to 1 in 2 people carry a gene that changes how they use folate. Here's the 1 sign you might."* (MTHFR)
5. **Visual prop explainer** (Humphrey Yang uses cash) → pour out *"this is 15g of fibre vs the 30g you need"* on the bench; or jars of sand for gut bacteria diversity.
6. **Identity cold open** (Vivian Tu) → *"If you're a woman over 40 who's exhausted no matter how much you sleep — this is for you."*
7. **"Stop doing X" command** (Tu, Ramit) → *"Stop taking magnesium at night if you have reflux. Do this instead."*
8. **Belief reframe** (Ramit's psychology) → *"'It's genetic' is not a life sentence. Here's what epigenetics actually means for your thyroid."*
9. **"Save this" reference carousel** (derm routines) → *"Save this: 5 labs your GP won't run that explain fatigue."*
10. **Open loop / "wait for it"** (all) → *"The one marker that explained her 10 years of fatigue… and it wasn't her thyroid."*
11. **Patient-arc storytelling** (Idriss) → anonymised 3-act case: rock bottom → the overlooked test → the turnaround.
12. **Tier list / ranking** (trend) → *"Ranking gut-health trends worst→best: fibremaxxing, colostrum, sea moss, L-glutamine."*
13. **Headline/study react** (Idz, Rajan) → react to a microplastics or seed-oil headline and add the nuance everyone missed.
14. **Label autopsy** (derm ingredient breakdowns; Hyman does this) → dissect a "healthy" supermarket product's label on camera.
15. **Named recurring series** (Huberman protocols, Bowe's "skin cycling") → launch a franchise: *"Myth Monday"*, *"Lab of the Week"*, or coin your own method (e.g. *"The Gut Reset Order"*).

### How to use it
- Pick ONE pattern + ONE topic per post. Don't blend.
- The first 1–2 seconds carry the whole video — lead with the hook line above, not an intro.
- Every post must pass the **credibility filter**: would a sceptical GP still respect you after watching? If no, kill it.
- Optimise for **saves + shares + DMs**, not raw views. Those are the qualified-reach signals that fill a clinic.
- Coin and repeat a **named method/series** — ownable language (like "skin cycling") is what makes an expert famous, not one-off virality.
